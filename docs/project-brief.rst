Project Brief
=============

Project name
------------

BC Native Plant & Pollinator Database (BC-NPPD).

Mission
-------

Create an evidence-based native plant and pollinator database for coastal
British Columbia, initially focused on low-growing species suitable for urban
environments.

Initial geographic focus
------------------------

* Metro Vancouver
* Fraser Valley
* Lower Mainland
* Coastal Douglas-fir overlap zones where relevant
* Coastal Western Hemlock urban contexts where relevant

Version 1.0.0a scope
--------------------

The foundation release should define the workbook/database architecture, lookup
tables, scoring frameworks, references, and the first fully researched flagship
species record.

Low-growing urban species definition
------------------------------------

For v1.0.0a and v1.0.0, prioritize species that generally meet these criteria:

* Native to southwest BC or broadly native in coastal BC.
* Mature height <= 60 cm, or maintainable below that threshold in urban
  settings.
* Suitable for urban gardens, boulevards, school grounds, pollinator strips,
  rain gardens, bioswales, or similar landscapes.
* Pollinator or wildlife value.
* Commercially available where possible.
* Non-invasive and appropriate for ecological planting contexts.

Design principles
-----------------

* Evidence first.
* One source of truth for each species.
* Unknown values remain blank or marked ``Unknown`` rather than guessed.
* Controlled vocabularies and documented scoring.
* Maintainable structure that can scale to hundreds of species.
